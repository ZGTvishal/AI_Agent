topic='Impact of AI on Cognitive Thinking of Human Beings' summary="The integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) into daily human life has initiated a significant shift in cognitive processes, primarily characterized by 'cognitive offloading.' This phenomenon occurs when individuals rely on AI tools to perform tasks that previously required mental effort, such as memory retrieval, analytical reasoning, and problem-solving. Research indicates a dual-natured impact: while AI can enhance efficiency and free up cognitive resources for higher-order creative thinking, excessive reliance may lead to the erosion of critical thinking skills, reduced memory consolidation, and a decline in independent logical reasoning. In educational contexts, AI is viewed both as a catalyst for fostering 21st-century skills (when used as a scaffold) and a risk to the development of foundational cognitive abilities (when used as a substitute). Furthermore, the use of generative AI influences the allocation of cognitive effort, potentially reducing the mental rigor required for complex tasks, which may lead to cognitive fatigue or a decrease in metacognitive awareness. Overall, the impact of AI on human cognition is not deterministic but depends heavily on the nature of the human-AI interaction and the degree of dependence on these systems." sources=['Devaki, V.
